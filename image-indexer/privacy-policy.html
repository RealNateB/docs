Jenkins – Privacy Policy

Effective date: February 14, 2026

Overview

Jenkins is a macOS photo and video indexing application developed by Nathan Brown. We take your privacy seriously. This policy explains what data Jenkins accesses and how it is handled.

Data Collection

Jenkins does not collect, transmit, or share any personal data. The app operates entirely on your local machine.

What Jenkins Accesses

Jenkins reads photo and video files from folders you explicitly select. It extracts metadata (file names, dates, camera info, GPS coordinates, and file hashes) to build a local index. Thumbnail images are generated and stored locally. All data remains on your computer in the application's support directory.

Network Access

Jenkins does not connect to the internet. No data is sent to any server, analytics service, or third party.

Data Storage

Index files and thumbnails are stored locally at ~/Library/Application Support/Jenkins/. You can delete this folder at any time to remove all app data.

Third-Party Services

Jenkins does not integrate with any third-party services, SDKs, or analytics platforms.

Changes to This Policy

If we update this policy, the revised version will be posted at this URL with an updated effective date.
